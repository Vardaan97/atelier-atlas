# Atelier Atlas - Dashboard Review Prompt

> Copy this prompt and give it to your Claude Chrome Extension while viewing the dashboard at localhost:3010

---

Review this interactive 3D globe dashboard (Atelier Atlas) from a UX/UI perspective. Analyze the following areas thoroughly:

## 1. User Experience
- Is the first-time user experience intuitive? Can someone figure out what to do without instructions?
- Are interactive elements (clickable countries, buttons, tabs) clearly discoverable?
- Is the information hierarchy clear?
- Are loading states and transitions smooth and informative?

## 2. Visual Design
- Is the dark theme consistent across all panels, tabs, and overlays?
- Are text sizes readable? Is there enough contrast?
- Does the glassmorphism style enhance or hinder usability?
- Are colors used effectively for data visualization?

## 3. Navigation & Information Architecture
- Is the tab system in the country panel intuitive?
- Are the left-side controls (metrics, filters, overlays) easy to understand?
- Is the search (Cmd+K) discoverable?
- Can users easily close panels and return to the globe?

## 4. Data Presentation
- Are statistics, charts, and trade data presented clearly?
- Are the radar charts, sparklines, and bar charts readable?
- Is the bottom ticker useful or distracting?
- Is the timeline slider intuitive?

## 5. Missing Best Practices
- Onboarding/tutorial for first-time users?
- Keyboard navigation support?
- Accessibility (screen readers, color blindness)?
- Empty states and error handling?
- Breadcrumbs or navigation trail?
- Share/export functionality?
- Favorites/bookmarks for countries?
- Help tooltip or legend for controls?
- Performance indicators (loading progress)?

## 6. Competitor Patterns
- What do Bloomberg Terminal, Google Earth, Our World in Data, or similar data dashboards do that this doesn't?
- Any standard dashboard patterns we're missing?

Provide specific, actionable improvement suggestions ranked by impact. Include both quick wins and larger feature ideas.
